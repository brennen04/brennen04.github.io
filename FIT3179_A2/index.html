The Next Chapter: Jobs, Pay, and Reality After University

In Australia, the transition from university to the workforce is a significant milestone that shapes the future of graduates. This visualization explores the relationship between university degrees, employment rates, and average salaries across various fields of study.

The Beginning: Graduation Is Only the Start

The end of university is the beginning of a bigger journey - one that leads into the workforce. But what does that path look like for thousands of Australian graduates every year?

Domestic graduates consistently get hired first across all degrees. Pursuing postgraduate coursework or research clearly boosts employment outcomes for domestic graduates, helping them secure jobs more easily. For international students, however, only postgraduate research significantly improves employment chances. The difference between domestic and international graduates narrows at the research level, showing that highly specialised skills can reduce the impact of citizenship status on employment.

Degrees That Open Doors: Full-Time Employment After Graduation

Some study paths lead straight into the workforce - others require a longer journey. Domestic graduates consistently achieve higher full-time employment across almost all study areas, often by 15-25 percentage points. Healthcare-related degrees perform strongly for both domestic and international students, with fields like Medicine, Pharmacy, and Rehabilitation offering reliable job prospects thanks to clear pathways and strong demand.

For international students, employment is generally lower - especially in Communications and Creative Arts , where success relies more on local networks. Still, there are bright spots: Dentistry sees international graduates outperform locals (93.3% vs 85.6%), and Tourism & Hospitality shows almost no gap (68.5% vs 69.8%). This contrast shows how the field you choose can shape the speed and ease of your transition into the workforce.
