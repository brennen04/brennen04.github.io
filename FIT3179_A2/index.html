The Next Chapter: Jobs, Pay, and Reality After University

In Australia, the transition from university to the workforce is a significant milestone that shapes the future of graduates. This visualization explores the relationship between university degrees, employment rates, and median salaries across various fields of study and universities.

The Beginning: Graduation Is Only the Start

The end of university marks the beginning of a bigger journey - one that leads into the workforce. But what does that path look like for thousands of Australian graduates every year?

Domestic graduates consistently get hired first across all degrees. Pursuing postgraduate coursework or research clearly boosts employment outcomes for domestic graduates, helping them secure jobs more easily. For international students, however, only postgraduate research significantly improves employment chances. The difference between domestic and international graduates narrows at the research level, showing that highly specialised skills can reduce the impact of citizenship status on employment.

Degrees That Open Doors: Full-Time Employment After Graduation

Some study paths lead straight into the workforce - others require a longer journey. Domestic graduates consistently achieve higher full-time employment across almost all study areas, often by 15-25 percentage points. Healthcare-related degrees perform strongly for both groups, with fields like Medicine, Pharmacy, and Rehabilitation offering reliable job prospects thanks to clear pathways and strong demand.

For international graduates international, employment is generally lower - especially in Communications and Creative Arts, where success relies more on local networks. Still, there are bright spots: Dentistry sees international graduates outperform locals (93.3% vs 85.6%), and Tourism & Hospitality shows almost no gap (68.5% vs 69.8%). This contrast shows how the field you choose can shape the speed and ease of your transition into the workforce.

Jobs: Mapping Australia's Hiring Hotspots

Vacancy rates show how many jobs are available compared to the labour force - a higher rate means more job openings per worker, often signalling strong demand or skill shortages. In 2024, The ACT (5.2%) and Northern Territory (3.8%) lead with the most opportunities, while larger states like NSW (2.1%) and Victoria (1.9%) have tighter, more competitive markets. Nationally, the rate is around 2.5% - roughly one unfilled job for every 40 workers.

University: Prestigeness, Reality

In 2024, Domestic graduates generally enjoy higher full-time employment (70-85%) and stronger median salaries ($70k-80k) across most universities. However, at Group of Eight (Go8) universities, prestige doesn't always guarantee success - undergraduate and international graduates often record lower employment rates (around 50-65%) and moderate salaries. This highlights a clear citizenship and institutional gap, where local experience, work rights, and regional demand often outweigh university brand in shaping graduate employability.

Does It Pay Off? Postgraduate Study Might Help

Higher education often boosts income - but not equally for everyone. In 2024, Domestic graduates see major gains, jumping from $75K at undergraduate to over $100K after postgraduate study. International students benefit most only through research degrees, highlighting that while advanced qualifications pay off, returns depend on citizenship and study type. This reflects how employability and income growth are tied to industry pathways and work experience.
